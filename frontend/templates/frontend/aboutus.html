Sounds shape the way we feel and see the world, yet we don’t often stop and listen, much less collect and share the sounds that surround us.

Do you remember the sound of dial-up internet? What about the whistle of an old kettle, the sound of the coffee machine at your favourite café, the no-sound of a silent elevator, or a wine cork popping. All of these sounds are associated with emotions and memory.

The Museum of Sound allows you to share sounds, as well as sharing your stories about why these sounds are meaningful to you.

Get involved by recording a short sound clip on the 'Record a Sound' page and telling us why this sound matters to you.

The Museum of Sound is curated and created by Dr. Sarah Collins, UWA and developed as a project for computer science students in CITS3200, 2018 and CITS5504, 2019-2020.
